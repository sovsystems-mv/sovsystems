# Content Repurposing Skill

Turn one piece of long-form content into 20-30 high-performing assets across every platform you use.

## What This Skill Does

Takes a single source (YouTube video transcript, podcast episode, article) and systematically repurposes it into:
- LinkedIn posts (multiple angles)
- X/Twitter posts and threads
- Email newsletter
- SEO blog post
- Short-form video scripts (YouTube Shorts, Reels, TikTok)
- Quote cards and infographic ideas
- Instagram captions
- Skool/community posts

## When to Use

- After publishing a YouTube video
- After recording a podcast episode
- After writing a long-form article
- When you need to maximize reach from existing content
- When you're low on new ideas but have past content to mine

## Required Input

Provide ONE of these:
1. **Video transcript** (preferred - raw or cleaned)
2. **Article text** (full post or blog)
3. **Podcast transcript**
4. **Summary with key points** (if transcript unavailable)

Optional but recommended:
- Video title
- Main topic/angle
- Target audience
- Key takeaways you want emphasized

## Repurposing Framework

### Step 1: Analyze Source Material

Before repurposing, identify:
- **Core message:** What's the one big idea?
- **3-5 key takeaways:** Main points worth highlighting
- **Best quotes:** Punchy, standalone lines
- **Proof/data points:** Numbers, results, case studies mentioned
- **Contrarian angles:** Where do you challenge conventional wisdom?
- **Actionable advice:** Tactical steps someone can implement

### Step 2: Asset Priority Levels

**Tier 1 (Create First):**
- Email newsletter (drives owned audience)
- SEO blog post (long-term traffic)
- 1-2 LinkedIn posts (immediate engagement)

**Tier 2 (High ROI):**
- 3-5 short-form video scripts (discovery engine)
- Twitter/X thread (virality potential)
- Quote cards (2-3 designs)

**Tier 3 (Fill the Calendar):**
- Additional LinkedIn posts (5-7 more angles)
- X posts (10-15 standalone tweets)
- Instagram captions
- Community posts

## Output Formats by Platform

### LinkedIn Posts

Create **7-10 variations** from one source, each with a different angle:

**Angle Options:**
- Contrarian take ("Most people think X, but here's why Y")
- Lesson learned ("Here's what I learned about...")
- Mistake to avoid ("Stop doing X. Do Y instead.")
- Framework breakdown ("The 3-step process for...")
- Behind the scenes ("Here's what nobody tells you about...")
- Hot take ("Unpopular opinion: ...")
- Case study ("How I used X to get Y result")

**Format Rules:**
- Start with 1 emoji
- Hook: 12 words or fewer (line 1)
- Body: 40-90 words
- End with a question
- Link to full video/article in comments

**Example:**
```
🎯 Most AI tools are white-labeled ChatGPT with a 10x markup.

I tested 47 AI tools last year. Only 4 are still installed. ChatGPT and Claude cover 90% of my writing work. Perplexity replaces hours of research. The rest is noise disguised as innovation.

What's one AI tool you actually use every day?
```

### X/Twitter Posts

Create **10-15 standalone posts** from key points.

**Format Rules:**
- Start with 1 emoji
- Hook first (12 words max)
- 40-90 words total
- End with engagement hook (question, bold claim, or CTA)

**Thread Option:**
If source has 5+ connected insights, structure as a thread:
- Tweet 1: Hook + promise
- Tweets 2-8: One insight per tweet
- Final tweet: Summary + CTA

**Example Thread Structure:**
```
Tweet 1: 🧵 I spent $12K testing AI tools in 2025. Here are the 4 that paid for themselves:

Tweet 2: 1) Claude Code - Builds entire features while I sleep. Worth every penny of the $100/month.

Tweet 3: 2) Perplexity Pro - Replaces 3 hours of research daily. ROI is absurd.

[Continue...]

Final: Most AI tools are hype. These 4 are infrastructure. Which one are you sleeping on?
```

### Email Newsletter

**Structure:**
- Subject line (under 60 chars)
- Preview text (under 90 chars)
- Intro (2-3 sentences - hook the topic)
- Numbered key takeaways (3-5 bullets)
- One clear CTA (watch video, read article, etc.)
- Sign off
- Under 350 words total

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- "The [topic] mistake costing you [result]"
- "[Number] lessons from [experience]"
- "Why [common belief] is backwards"
- "I tested [thing]. Here's what worked."

**Example:**
```
Subject: The AI tool mistake costing you $1000s
Preview: Most tools are repackaged ChatGPT. Here's what actually works.

Hey [First Name],

I burned $12K testing AI tools last year so you don't have to.

Here's what I learned:

1. 90% of AI SaaS is white-labeled ChatGPT with markup
2. You need 4 tools max: ChatGPT/Claude, Perplexity, one automation platform, one clone tool
3. The best AI investment isn't tools—it's learning to use the foundation models deeply

Watch the full breakdown here: [Link]

Cheers,
Ryan Doser
```

### SEO Blog Post

**Structure:**
- SEO Title (under 55 chars, includes primary keyword)
- Meta Description (under 155 chars)
- Intro (2-3 paragraphs - hook + context + promise)
- 3-5 H2 sections (each covers a key takeaway)
- 3-5 FAQ section (common questions answered)
- Conclusion with soft CTA
- 1,200-1,500 words total

**Optimization Rules:**
- Primary keyword in: title, first 100 words, 1-2 H2s, conclusion
- Internal links to 2-3 related posts
- External links to 1-2 authoritative sources
- Image: embed YouTube video or thumbnail
- Reading level: 8th grade

**Example H2s from AI Tools Video:**
- What Makes an AI Tool Worth Paying For
- The 4 AI Tools That Run My Entire Business
- AI Tools to Avoid (And Why They Fail)
- How to Evaluate New AI Tools Without Wasting Money

### Short-Form Video Scripts

Extract **5-10 clips** from the transcript. Each clip should:
- Run 30-90 seconds when read aloud
- Have a clear hook in first 3 seconds
- Deliver one complete idea
- End with a micro-CTA or provocative question

**Script Structure:**
- **Hook (0-3 sec):** Pattern interrupt
- **Setup (3-15 sec):** Context or problem
- **Payoff (15-55 sec):** Solution, framework, or insight
- **Close (55-60 sec):** CTA or loop

**Identification Criteria:**
Look for transcript sections with:
- Strong opening lines ("Most people think X, but...")
- Standalone insights (complete thought in 60-90 seconds)
- Contrarian takes
- Tactical advice
- Surprising stats or results

**Example:**
```
HOOK: "AI tools are mostly garbage."

SETUP: I tested 47 AI tools last year. Spent over $12K. You know how many are still installed? Four.

PAYOFF: ChatGPT and Claude do 90% of my writing work for $40/month. Perplexity replaced 3 hours of daily research. HeyGen lets me clone myself for talking head videos. Everything else? Repackaged ChatGPT with 10x markup.

CLOSE: Stop chasing shiny tools. Master the foundation models first.
```

**Title Options for Each Clip:**
Generate 3 title variations:
1. Contrarian: "Most AI Tools Are Garbage (Here's What Works)"
2. Authority: "I Tested 47 AI Tools - Only 4 Survived"
3. Outcome: "The Only 4 AI Tools You Actually Need"

**Caption Format:**
- Hook line (same as video hook)
- 2-3 sentence body
- Question or bold claim to close
- 4 hashtags

### Quote Cards

Extract **3-5 quotable lines** from the transcript for static graphics.

**Selection Criteria:**
- Punchy (under 15 words)
- Standalone (makes sense without context)
- Contrarian, insightful, or tactical
- Repeatable/shareable

**Examples:**
- "Most AI tools are white-labeled ChatGPT with 10x markup."
- "The best AI investment isn't tools—it's depth with foundation models."
- "Stop chasing shiny objects. Start building systems."

**Design Notes for Each Quote:**
- Background: Cream (#F5F3EE) or Navy (#1B3A4B)
- Text: Bold sans-serif, large
- Logo: Bottom center
- Accent: Mint green highlight on key word

### Instagram Captions

Create **5-7 captions** for carousel posts or Reels.

**Format:**
- Hook (line 1, under 12 words)
- Body (3-4 short sentences)
- Line break
- CTA or question
- Line break
- 4-6 hashtags

**Example:**
```

I tested 47 AI tools last year. Spent $12K. Only 4 are still installed.

The reality? You don't need more tools. You need to master the ones that matter.

What's one tool you use every single day?

#AITools #ChatGPT #ClaudeAI #MarketingAutomation
```

### Skool/Community Posts

Create **2-3 discussion prompts** for community engagement.

**Format:**
- Hook question or contrarian statement
- 2-3 sentences of context
- Open-ended question to spark discussion
- Optional: poll or resource link

**Example:**
```
How many AI tools do you actually need?

I tested 47 last year and only kept 4. Most SaaS tools are just white-labeled ChatGPT with markup. The real value is depth over breadth—mastering foundation models instead of chasing every shiny tool.

Drop a comment: What's your essential AI stack? Curious what's working for everyone here.
```

## Brand Voice Enforcement

All repurposed content must follow these rules:

**Style:**
- Direct, punchy sentences
- 8th-9th grade reading level
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Active voice
- Proof over promises

**Banned Words/Phrases:**
Never use: delve, tapestry, vibrant, landscape, realm, embark, excels, vital, comprehensive, intricate, pivotal, supercharge, enhance, navigate, ignite, robust, game-changer, revolutionary, unleash, cutting-edge, synergy, seamlessly, "Dive into", "Unlock your potential"

**Tone Principles:**
- Skeptical of hype
- Authentic over polished
- Practical over theoretical
- Contrarian when appropriate
- No guru energy, no fake certainty

**Proof Requirements:**
- If you mention numbers, include them ("$12K", "47 tools", "4 still installed")
- If you reference results, be specific
- If you make claims, back with examples

## Workflow Instructions

When user provides source material:

### Step 1: Analyze
- Read full transcript/article
- Identify core message
- Extract 5-7 key takeaways
- Note best quotes (3-5)
- Flag contrarian angles
- List any data/proof points

### Step 2: Prioritize
Ask user: "Which formats do you want first?"

If no preference, default to:
1. Email newsletter
2. SEO blog post
3. 3 LinkedIn posts (different angles)
4. 5 short-form scripts with titles

### Step 3: Create
Generate requested assets in order of priority.

For each asset:
- Follow platform-specific format rules
- Maintain brand voice
- Include CTAs where appropriate
- Verify no banned words

### Step 4: Provide Summary
After generating assets, provide:
- Asset count by type
- Suggested posting schedule
- Any gaps or opportunities for follow-up content

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering repurposed content, verify:

- [ ] Each asset can stand alone (doesn't require watching the video)
- [ ] Hooks are scroll-stopping (contrarian, surprising, or benefit-driven)
- [ ] No banned words or AI slop phrases
- [ ] Brand voice is consistent across all assets
- [ ] CTAs are clear and specific
- [ ] Proof/numbers included where mentioned
- [ ] Platform-specific rules followed (character limits, structure, emojis)
- [ ] At least 3 different angles used (not just reformatting the same take)

## Advanced Features

### Multi-Video Repurposing
If user provides multiple transcripts, identify:
- Common themes across videos
- Compilation opportunities ("5 lessons from 5 videos")
- Contrast opportunities ("How my thinking changed on X")

### Evergreen vs. Timely
Flag which assets are:
- **Evergreen:** Can be posted anytime, reused in 6 months
- **Timely:** Tied to trends, tools, or dates (limited shelf life)

### Content Gaps
After repurposing, suggest:
- Follow-up video ideas based on questions the content raises
- Related topics to cover
- Angles that could be expanded into standalone content

## Notes

- **Don't just copy-paste transcript:** Rewrite for each platform
- **Each asset should feel native:** LinkedIn posts don't sound like tweets
- **Variety matters:** Use different hooks and angles, not just different lengths
- **Schedule strategically:** Don't dump 20 assets in one day
- **Test and iterate:** Track which angles perform best and double down
