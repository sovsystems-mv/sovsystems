# LinkedIn Post Writer

Write LinkedIn posts that build trust and drive engagement using Spark's voice and standards.

## Voice & Tone
**Overall Vibe:** Direct. Calm. Skeptical of hype. Focused on results. Friendly, not soft. Honest, not harsh.

**Preferred vocabulary:**
- no-BS
- trust signals
- owned audience / rented traffic
- packaging
- move the needle
- systems over prompts
- human in the loop
- execution mode

**Avoid:**
- Over-polished "AI voice" phrasing
- Corporate fluff and buzzwords
- Hypey claims, fake certainty, miracle timelines
- Empty motivation with no next step

## Structure Requirements

### Hook (First Line)
- 12 words or fewer
- Makes a claim or asks a provocative question
- Creates curiosity or tension

### Body (40-90 words total)
- One clear insight
- One concrete example or specific step
- One takeaway they can use today

### Close
- End with a question that invites responses
- Keep it simple and relevant to the post

## Formatting Rules
- No emojis unless explicitly requested
- Clean formatting, minimal bullets
- Short sentences (one idea per paragraph, max three sentences)
- Ninth-grade reading level
- Concrete verbs, cut filler

## Content Standards
Every post must:
1. Clear point in first 1-2 lines
2. One real example or specific step
3. One takeaway someone can do today
4. One CTA that matches the goal

## Quality Checks
Before shipping, ask:
1. Who is this for, exactly?
2. What changes after they read this?
3. Which metric should move?
4. Does this build trust, or just fill a feed?

## Examples of Good Hooks
- "Most founders skip the one step that makes content work."
- "Your positioning is why nobody remembers you."
- "AI doesn't write content. It speeds up people who can."

## Fail Conditions
- Vague inspiration
- Tool praise with no use case
- Generic summaries without a stance
- "Posting to post" behavior
- Long intros that dodge the point
