# AI Rabbit Holes Podcast - Guest Research & Question Generation Skill

## Purpose
Research a podcast guest and generate high-quality interview questions following the AI Rabbit Holes podcast format with Intro, General Questions, and Rapid Fire Questions sections.

## When to Use
- Preparing for a new podcast episode
- Researching a potential guest
- Creating interview prep documents

## Required Inputs
- **Guest Name**: Full name of the podcast guest
- **Guest Context** (optional): Any specific topics, recent work, or angles to focus on

## Process

### Step 1: Guest Research
Research the guest thoroughly using web search:

1. **Background & Credentials**
   - Current role/company
   - Notable achievements
   - Areas of expertise
   - Past podcast appearances or talks

2. **Recent Work & Content**
   - Latest blog posts, videos, or social content
   - Recent interviews or public statements
   - Tools, frameworks, or methodologies they've created
   - Specific claims or data points they've shared

3. **Social Presence**
   - Twitter/X, LinkedIn, YouTube handles
   - Website or newsletter
   - Community or course offerings

4. **Unique Angles**
   - Contrarian takes or controversial opinions
   - Specific experiments or case studies
   - What makes their approach different

Use this exact structure:

## General Questions

[8-15 open-ended questions with these characteristics:]

- Reference their actual work, past statements, or data
- Include sub-questions marked with dashes for follow-ups
- Add parenthetical references for sources when applicable
- Mix strategic (big picture) and tactical (specific how-to) questions

## Style Guidelines

### Voice & Tone
- **Direct & conversational**: "Let's be real...", "So here's the thing..."
- **Specific & credible**: Reference their actual work, not generic questions
- **Contrarian-lite**: Gently challenge assumptions without being hostile
- **System-focused**: Ask about workflows and frameworks, not just tools
- **Practical**: Always aim for actionable frameworks

### Question Starters to Use
- "You've said/mentioned/noted that..."
- "Let's talk about..."
- "Walk us through..."
- "What's your take on..."
- "Explain how..."
- "What changed?"
- "Can you elaborate on..."

### Formatting
- **Bold** for section headers and key phrases
- *Italic* for emphasized words or concepts
- "Quotation marks" around claimed statements
- (Parentheses) for supporting context or sources
- Dashes (-) for sub-questions

### Question Quantity
- General Questions: 8-15 questions
- Rapid Fire Questions: 6-10 questions
- Total: 14-25 questions per guest

Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Guest bio is accurate and specific (not generic)
- [ ] Questions reference their actual work/statements
- [ ] Mix of strategic and tactical questions included
- [ ] At least one screenshare opportunity (if applicable)
- [ ] Rapid fire questions are punchy and quotable
- [ ] Closing question asks where to follow them
- [ ] No fluff or generic filler questions
- [ ] Questions align with AI/marketing/systems focus of the podcast
